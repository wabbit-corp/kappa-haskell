## Coverage and honest rejection

The backend compiles the accepted run-mode (`UIO`) surface exercised by the
in-tree conformance suite and seven adversarial review rounds â€” no
spec-mandated *runtime* construct it touches is rejected or silently
miscompiled. (`using` desugars to a bind in elaboration; Â§21 symbol / Â§23
staging reflection live in the `Elab` monad and so never appear in a `UIO`
program; algebraic-effect `handle` is `E_UNSUPPORTED` upstream in the
interpreter itself, so it never reaches the backend.) The
`E_BACKEND_UNSUPPORTED` mechanism remains a **defensive, fail-stop backstop**,
emitted with a source span and **no** executable, never a silent fallback: it
fires for genuinely non-runtime forms (a bare un-eta-expanded constructor or a
`KUsing` item â€” elaboration invariants â€” and the `Elab`-only reflection
prims), and now also for a real value-typed global that reaches codegen with
no recorded core body â€” surfacing such a gap honestly instead of erasing it
to unit. (Two such latent gaps â€” prefixed-binding and `projection` globals â€”
were found by the review rounds and fixed at the source by recording their
bodies; the guard is the backstop, `recordCoreBody` is the fix.) The
no-fallback property is exercised by the namespace-hygiene, the build-side
foreign-capability (no `--ffi-full`), and the interpreter foreign-`expect`
native-suite sections.

Out of native-backend scope (a *shared* interpreter+backend behaviour, not a
native divergence): a Â§17.2.3 binding or-pattern whose alternatives bind the
same names in different field *positions* (`A p q | B q p`) is wired
positionally by the elaborator, so native and interpreter agree (both follow
the first alternative's name order). That is an elaborator/Â§17.2.3 question,
not a backend conformance defect â€” the native output matches the interpreter
oracle.

## Documented representation limits (not Â§27.5A.3 violations; fail-stop)

Genuinely non-tail recursion is bounded by the C stack (the interpreter is
also O(n) stack here, and `ulimit -s` raises the bound; never a wrong
answer): a recursive call in the **bound leg** of `bindIO`/`ioThen` /
left-nested `>>=`; a deeply-recursive `!`-splice; an `if`-branch recursion
that is not the do-block's last item. Multi-argument **mutual** recursion
trampolines through the curried closure (1 closure allocation per step;
self- and arity-1 recursion are allocation-free) â€” correct and
constant-stack, with a clear future fix (a multi-arg worker bounce).
